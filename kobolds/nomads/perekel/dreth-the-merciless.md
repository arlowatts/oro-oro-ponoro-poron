Dreth the Merciless is a mythological figure on Oro-oro-ponoro-poron, depicted as a powerful and dangerous druid. Their
story, passed down in countless forms by oral tradition, describes the terrible consequences of {% a Ice %}'s curse brought
about by their own greed.

The story was recorded and published by a historian in the {% a Bekebel %} clan, as told to them by an elder of the
Perekel clan. This version is duplicated below.

## Dreth the Merciless and Ice's Curse

Be afraid of Dreth the Merciless, and do not repeat their mistakes! They lived many centuries ago, when we still
worshiped the wyverns of the jungle. Dreth, having great ambition, leveraged their druidic powers to gain influence and
control in our clan, eventually supplanting our leader and establishing a tyrannical rule. But that was not enough for
Dreth the Merciless. They sought greater and greater power, never satisfied with what they had. Then, during the longest
night of the year, they discovered the shadow dragon {% a Ice %} resting in a shallow cave. Dreth addressed the dragon.

> Ice, dragon of shadow. I am Dreth the Merciless, the greatest druid to have ever lived. Will you not grant me some of
> your power?

Ice replied,

> You have ambition but not strength. You cannot have my power.

Frustrated, Dreth returned home. They honed their magical powers for a full year, increasing their abilities tenfold.
Then, again during the longest night of the year, Dreth returned to the same site and found the dragon waiting for them.
Again they addressed Ice.

> Ice, dragon of shadow. I have gazed upon lands no other eyes have seen and formed spells no other hand has cast. I am
> great but I could be greater. Will you not grant your power to me?

And again Ice replied,

> Your body is strong and your mind is stronger, but you offer me nothing. I will not give you power.

Dreth was angry, but knew they had no choice. They resolved to offer a gift of unmatched value to Ice, and swore to pass
the dragon's cryptic test once and for all. They spent a year crafting an artifact imbued with untold magic. When it was
finished, they returned to Ice's shallow cave and addressed the dragon.

> Ice, dragon of shadow. I bring you an artifact of my own creation. It is an offering to you and a demonstration of my
> power, in exchange for yours.

This time, Ice thought for a moment. Then it said,

> You have demonstrated your power and proven your devotion. I will give you my power, but you must also accept my
> curse.

Desperate for the final reward of their years-long trial, Dreth agreed instantly. Their vision suddenly darkened and
their strength fled their body. They collapsed in the mouth of the cave, and slept through until morning.

When they awoke, both Ice and the artifact had gone and Dreth began to walk home. As they trudged weakly through the
forest, they found that the thick undergrowth and tangling vines no longer hindered their progress; they moved through
them as though through shallow water. Knowing this to be the strength of Ice, they continued. The longer they walked,
the more uninhibited they became. As they stepped lightly through the jungle, their strides became longer and longer
until their feet hardly touched the ground. In their elation, they hardly noticed that their vision was quickly
narrowing.

Soon they could see nothing at all, but did not know it for they had closed their eyes against the rushing air. They
flew now, on dragon's wings, and the land itself moved aside to let them pass. As they glided through the jungle, they
felt a welling in their chest and exhaled black fire before them. But they did not know that they had reached our camp,
and the black fire killed many of Dreth's own people before they finally passed through and disappeared into the dense
canopy. Then, lost in the joy of flight, Dreth soared for many years before they finally opened their eyes. When they did,
they called themself Ice and remembered nothing of their past or of the Perekel clan.
