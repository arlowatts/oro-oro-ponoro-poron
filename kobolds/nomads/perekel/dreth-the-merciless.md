Dreth the Merciless is a mythological figure on Oro-oro-ponoro-poron, depicted as a powerful and dangerous druid. Her
story, passed down in countless forms by oral tradition, describes the terrible consequences of {% a Ice %}'s curse
brought about by her own greed.

The version of the story given below was recorded and published by a historian in the {% a Bekebel %} clan, as told to
them by a Perekel storyteller.

## Dreth the Merciless and Ice's Curse

Be afraid of Dreth the Merciless, and do not repeat her mistakes! She lived many centuries ago, when we still worshiped
the wyverns of the jungle. Dreth, having great ambition, leveraged her druidic powers to gain influence and control in
our clan, eventually supplanting our leader and establishing a tyrannical rule. But that was not enough for Dreth the
Merciless. She sought greater and greater power, never satisfied with what she had. Then, during the longest night of
the year, she discovered the shadow dragon {% a Ice %} resting in a shallow cave. Dreth addressed the dragon.

> Ice, dragon of shadow. I am Dreth the Merciless, the greatest druid to have ever lived. Will you not grant me some of
> your power?

Ice replied,

> You have ambition but not strength. You cannot have my power.

Frustrated, Dreth returned home. She honed her magical powers for a full year, increasing her abilities tenfold. Then,
again during the longest night of the year, Dreth returned to the same site and found the dragon waiting for her. Again
she addressed Ice.

> Ice, dragon of shadow. I have gazed upon lands no other eyes have seen and formed spells no other hand has cast. I am
> great but I could be greater. Will you not grant your power to me?

And again Ice replied,

> Your body is strong and your mind is stronger, but you offer me nothing. I will not give you power.

Dreth was angry, but knew she had no choice. She resolved to offer a gift of unmatched value to Ice, and swore to pass
the dragon's cryptic test once and for all. She spent a year crafting an artifact imbued with untold magic, greater than
any that had ever been made on Oro-oro-ponoro-poron. When it was finished, she returned to Ice's shallow cave and
addressed the dragon.

> Ice, dragon of shadow. I bring you an artifact of my own creation. It is an offering to you and a demonstration of my
> power, in exchange for yours.

This time, Ice thought for a moment. Then it said,

> You have demonstrated your power and proven your devotion. I will give you my power, but you must also accept my
> curse.

Desperate for the final reward of her years-long trial, Dreth agreed instantly. Her vision suddenly darkened and
strength fled her body. She collapsed in the mouth of the cave, and slept until morning.

When she awoke, both Ice and the artifact had gone and Dreth began to walk home. As she trudged weakly through the
forest, she found that the thick undergrowth and tangling vines no longer hindered her progress; she moved through them
as though through shallow water. Knowing this to be the strength of Ice, she continued. The longer she walked, the more
uninhibited she became. As she stepped lightly through the jungle, her strides became longer and longer until her feet
hardly touched the ground. In her elation, she hardly noticed that her vision was quickly narrowing.

Soon she could see nothing at all, but did not know it for she had closed her eyes against the rushing air. She flew
now, on dragon's wings, and the land itself moved aside to let her pass. As she glided through the jungle, she felt a
welling in her chest and exhaled black fire before her. But she did not know that she had reached our camp, and the
black fire killed many of our people before Dreth finally passed through and disappeared into the dense canopy. Then,
lost in the joy of flight, Dreth soared for many years before she finally opened her eyes. When she did, she called
herself Ice and remembered nothing of her past or of the Perekel clan.
